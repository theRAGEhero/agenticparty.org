How it works

Small machines that turn participation into decisions.

Each panel is a working sketch of one capability — how the platform is hosted, how it gathers people, how contributions become teams and decisions, and how all of it stays legible.

{{/* 01 topology */}}
01

Where it runs

One party on its own platform, many parties hosted together on shared infrastructure, or a federation of independent platforms that interlink — the same software, deployed to fit the political reality.

deploy-topology.sh

mode: SINGLE // one party · one dedicated platform

Three deployment options — Single: one party on one dedicated platform; Shared: many parties on one multi-tenant platform; Federated: each party runs its own platform, interlinked.

{{/* 02 reach */}}
02

It gathers people

Citizens and elected representatives converge on one place. The platform's job is to make joining easy and keep representation connected to the people it actually speaks for.

reach.sh

signal: citizens dock onto the platform · every fifth is a linked representative.

{{/* 03 synthesize */}}
03

Listening without flattening

Thousands of raw contributions don't have to become mush. The platform clusters them into clear themes while keeping minority and dissenting voices visibly attached — nothing gets averaged away.

synthesize.sh

signal: themes emerge · dissent stays visible, not averaged away.

{{/* 04 converge */}}
04

It turns positions into teams

Where people already agree, the platform surfaces the convergence and helps it become a real working group instead of staying scattered.

converge.sh

signal: shared positions cluster into named working groups.

{{/* 05 deliberate */}}
05

Disagreement, organized

Conflict is not the enemy of a healthy party — unstructured conflict is. Arguments for and against a question sort into a legible structure instead of a flame war.

deliberate.sh

signal: pro and con, structured around the question — not a flame war.

{{/* 06 delegate */}}
06

A voice you can lend — and reclaim

Delegate your vote on a topic to someone you trust, and take it straight back whenever you want. Representation becomes flexible and revocable, topic by topic.

delegate.sh

signal: lend your voice on a topic · reclaim it anytime.

{{/* 07 trace */}}
07

Listening leaves a trace

Every contribution can be followed all the way through: raised by a citizen, shaping a decision, and returning as a visible outcome. Participation you can actually track.

trace.sh

signal: contribution → decision → visible outcome, back to the citizen.

{{/* 08 ledger */}}
08

It stays honest

Every consequential action — a vote, a fund, a decision — is signed, timestamped and public. Tampering with the record is detected and rejected, never quietly hidden.

public-ledger.sh

signal: an append-only public record · edits to the past are rejected.

{{/* 09 roles */}}
09

A person is more than an elector

The same citizen can vote, contribute, organize, join a working group and hold leadership to account — often at once. Agency accumulates; it isn't a single box to tick.

roles.sh

signal: one person, many roles — accumulating, not either/or.

{{/* 10 collective */}}
10

The whole is smarter than its parts

Put it together and something larger appears. The platform sits between the people and the party, turning thousands of separate contributions into shared intelligence — and carrying the party's direction back to the people.

collective.sh

signal: the people + the party, mediated by the platform — collective intelligence.

{{/* 11 celebrate */}}
11

A party people are glad to join

All of this has one human payoff. When listening leaves a trace and participation actually moves things, belonging to a political community feels good again — not a chore to endure, but something citizens are happy to show up for.

celebrate.sh

signal: participation that people actually enjoy.
